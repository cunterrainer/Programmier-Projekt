Time, an ever-present force in our lives, is a concept that has fascinated humanity for centuries. It governs our daily routines, dictates the progression of our lives, and is a central theme in both science and philosophy. Yet, despite its omnipresence, time remains an elusive and enigmatic phenomenon. Philosophers have long debated its nature: is time a linear progression from past to future, or is it cyclical, repeating itself endlessly? Some argue that time is an illusion, a construct of human consciousness that helps us make sense of our experiences. Others contend that time is a fundamental aspect of the universe, woven into the very fabric of reality
